Diabetic foot complications represent one of the most debilitating and costly consequences of diabetes mellitus, often leading to chronic ulceration and lower-limb amputation. Conventional clinical monitoring is inherently limited by infrequent patient visits, and affected individuals frequently lack protective pain sensation due to peripheral neuropathy, leaving pathological conditions undetected until irreversible damage occurs. This paper presents the design and implementation of an integrated smart insole system — a compact, wearable Cyber-Physical Alert System (CPAS) — engineered specifically for the early detection and prevention of diabetic foot ulcers. The proposed system embeds five complementary sensors within a flexible insole: an NTC thermistor for real-time skin temperature monitoring, a DHT22 sensor for intra-shoe humidity and ambient temperature measurement, a Velostat-based pressure mapping array for plantar pressure distribution, an MPU6050 inertial measurement unit for gait analysis and abnormal motion detection, and a MAX30102 optical sensor for peripheral blood flow estimation. The system's cyber layer integrates a feedback-driven control algorithm that continuously correlates multi-parameter data against clinically validated thresholds. Experimental validation demonstrates a system response latency of under one second — a reduction of more than 80% compared to conventional periodic clinical assessment. The entire system was realized at a component cost below 70 USD, establishing a highly cost-effective platform suitable for home use and large-scale preventive healthcare deployment.
